2. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE OF THIS MANUAL

2.1 This manual is published in accordance with Section 51 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000 (“PAIA”). PAIA gives effect to the constitutional right of access to information held by private bodies that is required for the exercise or protection of any rights.

2.2 The purpose of this manual is to assist potential requesters by describing:

  • (a) The categories of records held by Skootlink (in respect of its product Crosssa);
  • (b) How a request for access to such records can be made;
  • (c) The fees and procedures applicable to requests;
  • (d) The types of records automatically available without a formal PAIA request.

2.3 This manual must be read together with the Crosssa Privacy Policy and Crosssa Terms of Service, which contain further information regarding the processing and protection of personal information under the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (“POPIA”).

7. PROCEDURE FOR REQUESTING ACCESS TO RECORDS

7.1 A person who wishes to request access to a record held by Skootlink must:

  • (a) Complete the prescribed Form C (Request for Access to Record of Private Body), which is available from the Information Regulator’s website at https://inforegulator.org.za/paia-forms/ or from the Department of Justice website;
  • (b) Provide sufficient detail on the form to enable the Information Officer to identify the requested record (including, where possible, the nature of the record, the date it was created, and any relevant reference numbers);
  • (c) Identify the right that the requester seeks to exercise or protect, and explain why the record is required for the exercise or protection of that right;
  • (d) Specify the form in which access is required (e.g., inspection, copy, or electronic format);
  • (e) Provide a postal or email address for correspondence;
  • (f) Submit the completed form together with the prescribed request fee (see section 8 below) to the Information Officer.

7.2 The request may be submitted by: Email: help@skootlink.co.za

7.3 The Information Officer will acknowledge receipt of the request within fourteen (14) days and will notify the requester of the decision regarding the request within thirty (30) days of receipt.

7.4 The thirty day period may be extended once for a further period of not more than thirty (30) days if the request is for a large volume of records, or if the request requires a search through records held by another body. The requester will be notified in writing of any such extension.

8. FEES

8.1 Request Fee: A request fee is payable upon submission of a PAIA request. The fee is prescribed by the Minister of Justice and published in the Government Gazette. The Information Officer will advise the requester of the applicable fee upon receipt of the request.

8.2 Access Fee: If the request is granted, an access fee may be payable. The access fee covers the cost of searching for, reproducing, and preparing the record for delivery. The applicable fees are set out in the PAIA Regulations and will be communicated to the requester before access is granted.

8.3 Personal Information of the Requester: No fee is payable for a request for access to a record containing personal information about the requester (i.e., the person making the request).

8.4 A schedule of the current prescribed fees is available from the Information Regulator or on request from the Information Officer.

9. GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL OF ACCESS

9.1 Skootlink may refuse a request for access to a record in whole or in part on any of the following grounds, as set out in PAIA:

  • (a) Mandatory Protection of Privacy: The record contains personal information about a third party (including another user) and disclosure would involve the unreasonable disclosure of personal information about that third party.
  • (b) Mandatory Protection of Commercial Information: The record contains trade secrets, financial, commercial, scientific, or technical information that would likely cause harm to the commercial or financial interests of Skootlink or a third party.
  • (c) Mandatory Protection of Confidential Information: The record is privileged from production in legal proceedings, or its disclosure would constitute a breach of a duty of confidence owed to a third party.
  • (d) Mandatory Protection of Research Information: The record relates to research being carried out by or on behalf of Skootlink and disclosure would expose the researcher or the subject matter to serious disadvantage.
  • (e) Mandatory Protection of Safety and Property: Disclosure of the record would be likely to endanger the life or physical safety of an individual or prejudice the security of property.
  • (f) Other Grounds: Any other ground for refusal recognised by PAIA, including where the record is already publicly available, the request is vexatious or frivolous, or the work required to process the request would substantially and unreasonably divert the resources of Skootlink.

9.2 If a request is refused, the Information Officer will provide written reasons for the refusal and advise the requester of the remedies available under PAIA, including the right to apply to court for relief.

10. REMEDIES

10.1 If a requester is dissatisfied with the Information Officer’s decision regarding a request, the requester may, within thirty (30) days of the decision:

  • (a) Apply to the Information Regulator for conciliation or mediation; or
  • (b) Apply to a competent court for appropriate relief.

10.2 A requester who is dissatisfied with the level of fees charged may, within the prescribed period, apply to the Information Regulator to review the fees.
